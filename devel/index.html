The Saturn V Rocket S-IC (first stage of the Saturn V, comprised of five F-1 Engines) used at liftoff for the Apollo Missons is so far the most powerful rocket used in human history with 7.6 million pounds of thrust.

Wolf 1061c

Wolf 1061c orbits a red dwarf and possibly has 1.6 times Earth's gravity. It has an equilibrium temperature of 223K (close to Mars, -50C or -58F) and is extremely close to its parent star, meaning it may be tidally locked. If it is tidally locked, life can only exist on the sides of the planet.

  • Distance: 13.8 ly
  • Orbit: 17.9 days
  • Mass: 4.3x Earth

Kepler 442b

Kepler 442b orbits a K type star (orange dwarf) and possibly has gravity 30% stronger than Earth. Located near its parent star, rotaional periods (days) are much slower and one day on Kepler 442b could be equivalent to weeks or months on Earth.

  • Distance: 1,120 ly
  • Orbit: 113 days
  • Mass: 2.3x Earth

Kepler 186f

Kepler 186f orbits a red dwarf, it has an equilibrium tempature of 188K (colder than Mars, -85C or -121F), and its gravity is currently unknown. Like Kepler 442b its rotational period is extremely slow and one day could be equivalent to weeks or months, although it may be tidally locked as well. A climate model suggests that if at least .5 to 5 bars of carbon dioxide exists then its surface tempature could be 273K (much like Earth, 0C or 32F).

  • Distance: 560 ly
  • Orbit: 130 days
  • Mass: 1.4x Earth

GJ 667Cc

GJ 667Cc orbits a red dwarf and may have suitable equilibrium tempatures at 277K (4C or 39F). It most likely is tidally locked, however if it has a thicker atmosphere, the side facing away from its host star could be habitable from heat transfer.

  • Distance: 23.6 ly
  • Orbit: 28 days
  • Mass: 3.7x Earth

Kepler 62f

Kepler 62f orbits a K-type (orange dwarf) star and may have an equilibrium temperature of 208K (-65C or -85F). The planet may be a water world, meaning that the majority of the planet may be covered in water (or ice if not enough carbon dioxide exists, although based on studies, Kepler 62f most likely has an atmosphere with enough carbon dioxide). It may also have its own satellite like our own moon, meaning the planet may have tides much like Earth. It's rotaional period and axial tilt is also very similar to Earth. Kepler 62f sounds like another Earth, but unfortunately is 1,200 light years away.

  • Distance: 1,200 ly
  • Orbit: 267 days
  • Mass: Possibly 2.8x Earth

Kepler 438b

Kepler 438b orbits a red dwarf and has an equilibrium temperature of 276K (3C or 37F). It is most likely tidally locked and a study rates Kepler 438b as the most Earth-like planet in terms of physics and chemistry. Unforunately, the planet gets powerful radiation storms every 100 days, capable of sterlizing life on the planet.

  • Distance: 470 ly
  • Orbit: 35 days
  • Mass: .5x Earth
